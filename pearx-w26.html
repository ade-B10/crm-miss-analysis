Executive Summary

PearX W26 presented 13 companies spanning healthcare delivery, fintech compliance, AI infrastructure, edtech, vertical SaaS, and logistics. The batch skews heavily toward AI-native products (7 of 13 companies) with several targeting large traditional industries — aligning with Base10's "automation of the real economy" thesis.

The standout is Pronto (5/5) — last-mile pharmacy delivery with $50K MRR growing 25%/month, operating in a $500B+ US pharmacy market. The team (Amazon Pharmacy / DoorDash / Capsule alumni) has deep domain expertise. Reflex and Titan (both 4/5) also warrant immediate follow-up given their traction and fit with Base10's fintech and data infrastructure themes.

Key Takeaway: 4 of 13 companies have strong Base10 fit (Pronto, Reflex, Titan, Forge) with real economy automation and AI-powered vertical software. Three companies (Pronto, Reflex, Forge) are applying technology to massive traditional industries with domain-expert founders — Base10's sweet spot. Recommend immediate outreach on Pronto and Reflex through existing Pejman relationship.

Base10 Fit

Product: Pronto provides same-day and on-demand delivery infrastructure for independent pharmacies, enabling them to compete with Amazon Pharmacy and CVS. The platform handles routing, dispatch, real-time tracking, and pharmacy workflow integration. Think "DoorDash for prescriptions" but purpose-built for pharmacy compliance requirements (HIPAA, controlled substances, temperature-sensitive meds).

Market: The US pharmacy market exceeds $500B annually. Independent pharmacies (21,000+ locations) are losing ground to Amazon Pharmacy and mail-order. Last-mile delivery is the #1 requested feature from patients, but most independents lack the logistics infrastructure to offer it. Pronto solves this with a turnkey solution.

Base10 Fit Analysis: This is a textbook Base10 deal. Technology (AI routing + logistics optimization) applied to a massive traditional industry (pharmacy/healthcare), with domain-expert founders from Amazon Pharmacy and DoorDash. The 25% MoM growth at $50K MRR suggests strong product-market fit. The real economy automation thesis is perfectly aligned — Pronto is digitizing a fragmented, offline supply chain. Recommend: Immediate follow-up through Pejman.

Product: Reflex deploys AI agents that automate KYC (Know Your Customer) and AML (Anti-Money Laundering) compliance workflows for financial institutions. Instead of armies of compliance analysts manually reviewing documents and flagging suspicious transactions, Reflex's agents handle the entire workflow — document verification, sanctions screening, transaction monitoring, and SAR filing preparation.

Market: Global compliance spending exceeds $270B annually, with KYC/AML representing the largest segment. Banks spend $60M-$500M per year on compliance staff alone. Regulatory pressure is only increasing (FinCEN, EU AML directives). The market is massive, growing, and desperately needs automation — current solutions are mostly rule-based legacy systems.

Base10 Fit Analysis: Excellent fit. This is AI automating a massive, manual, regulation-driven process in financial services — pure "real economy automation." The Goldman/Palantir team has deep domain credibility. 3x growth in 6 months at $400K ARR suggests the product is working. The regulatory moat (compliance software requires deep domain expertise and certification) creates defensibility. This sits squarely in Base10's fintech thesis. Recommend: Priority follow-up. Request intro through Pejman this week.

Lessons from CRM Miss Analysis Applied

Cross-referencing the PearX W26 batch against patterns identified in the CRM Miss Analysis.

Pattern Check: AI Dev Tools TAM (Basalt, Arcwise)

The CRM Miss Analysis identified "market sizing — AI dev tools TAM unclear" as a recurring pass reason (Wordware, Cartesia). Basalt and Arcwise both sit in this category. While neither is a priority, they should go on the 6-month re-check list per Recommendation #3 from the miss analysis.

Pattern Check: "Too Early" Bias (Haven, Gleam, Junction)

Three companies (Haven, Gleam, Junction) were rated 2/5, likely partly on traction maturity. The miss analysis found that "too early" passes accounted for 2 of 15 misses, with companies like Cartesia breaking out 6-12 months after a pass. All three should get a structured 6-month re-evaluation.

Pattern Check: Domain Expert Founders (Pronto, Forge, Gleam)

The miss analysis identified "domain-expert founders building vertical AI products" as the most common thread in Tier 1 misses. Three PearX companies (Pronto, Forge, Gleam) have domain-expert founding teams. Pronto and Forge are already flagged as priority; Gleam deserves more attention than the 2/5 rating suggests based on this pattern.

Pattern Check: Competitive Analysis Depth (Cinder, Delphi)

Both Cinder and Delphi have well-known company names in adjacent spaces (Cinder the Meta T&S platform, Delphi the AI clone platform). Per the miss analysis's surface-level competitive analysis pattern, ensure any pass decision is based on the actual PearX company's product and customers, not a name-based assumption about competitive overlap.
