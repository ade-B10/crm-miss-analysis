What Is Consensus? Definitions & Thresholds

The CRM Miss Analyzer uses a specific, measurable definition of "consensus" to identify deals Base10 evaluated but did not invest in that later became breakout companies. Here are the three core criteria:

$25M+
Minimum Round Size
The company must have raised a subsequent round of $25M or more. This filters out companies that merely survived and isolates those that attracted significant capital conviction.
Top 30
Lead Investor Tier
The round must have been led by a top-30 venture firm. This ensures the company didn't just raise from anyone but attracted the most competitive, signal-heavy capital.
Series A+
Minimum Stage
The round must be Series A or later, confirming the company achieved sufficient product-market fit and traction to clear institutional underwriting bars.

Why This Definition Matters

The threshold is intentionally conservative. We only count a miss if: (1) Base10 had a documented discussion, (2) there was an explicit pass or deprioritization decision, (3) the subsequent round was $25M+ from a top-30 firm, and (4) the timing confirms we could have invested at the point of discussion. This avoids inflating the miss count with companies we never seriously evaluated or that raised from non-signal investors.

The goal is learning, not blame. The question is always: "what market signal did we misread?" not "who made a bad call." By holding to a $25M+ / top-30 firm bar, we ensure every identified miss represents a genuine blind spot worth analyzing.

What the Team Is Missing

DD process not yet established. No dedicated Slack channel, no customer calls scheduled, no competitive deep-dive assigned. Compare to other active deals (SuperDial, Axle) which have established #early-deal channels and multi-week DD plans. Rime could easily slip to deprioritization (Pattern 6).

If Rime's phonemic accuracy becomes the standard for regulated voice work (medical, finance, pharma), the TAM and defensibility expand dramatically beyond current positioning.

Analysis of Base10's historical misses reveals 7 recurring patterns. These are not "bad decisions" but systematic blind spots that, when recognized in real-time, should trigger additional scrutiny before passing.

Pattern 1: "Category Is Sucky" / Static TAM Framing

MEDIUM PREDICTOR

What it looks like: Team sizes the TAM based on the company's current product and ICP, concludes the market is crowded or the wedge is too small. Fails to account for the company becoming the infrastructure layer that all competitors depend on.

Application to Rime: Voice AI is a hot but genuinely crowded space. Risk is that the framing becomes "just another TTS company competing with ElevenLabs" rather than "enterprise phonemic infrastructure layer that differentiates all voice systems." If sized as point solution, TAM looks $2-3B. If sized as infrastructure moat, TAM is $25B+.

Dani's sourcing was excellent. Identified Rime via John at Unusual Ventures, flagged in pipeline mid-March. Initial data on founder pedigree (Stanford NLP PhD, Amazon Alexa team) and traction ($3M ARR) was comprehensive.

Rexhi's March 25 meeting was substantive. Met Lily, Nick, and Ares. Data room shared via DocSend. Feedback positive ("was great touching base again today").

Market mapping acknowledged competitive intensity. ElevenLabs, Deepgram, Play.ht, OpenAI TTS all understood as active players. Correctly identified that voice AI is crowded space.

What the Team Is Missing

Production scale evidence underweighted. 100M+ phone conversations monthly, 1M+ requests per day, 20M outbound sales calls this quarter are not "pilot signals." These are production-scale deployments. This evidence would typically clear conviction bars for Series A at comparable ARR multiples.

Enterprise moat vs. consumer moat confusion. Risk of conflating Rime with ElevenLabs/Play.ht (consumer TTS) when Rime's phonemic pronunciation layer targets enterprise phone systems (Domino's, Wingstop). These are different go-to-markets and defensibility profiles.

Growth acceleration discounted as "too recent." $1.5M in last 2 months of a $3M total is 50% of all-time revenue in ~60 days. This is precisely the inflection signal Series A investors chase. Waiting for another quarter of data means missing the entry window.

DD process not yet established. No dedicated Slack channel, no customer calls scheduled, no competitive deep-dive assigned. Compare to other active deals (SuperDial, Axle) which have established #early-deal channels and multi-week DD plans. Rime could easily slip to deprioritization (Pattern 6).

The Core Question the Team Isn't Asking

The team may be sizing the point solution (speech-to-speech model accuracy for enterprise calls) when the platform opportunity is much larger (AI infrastructure for all voice use cases: customer service, sales calls, IVR, internal comms). If Rime's phonemic accuracy becomes the standard for regulated voice work (medical, finance, pharma), the TAM and defensibility expand dramatically beyond current positioning.

B10 Miss Patterns That Predict Consensus

Analysis of Base10's historical misses reveals 7 recurring patterns. These are the internal reasoning patterns that, when present at the time of a pass decision, have historically preceded the company becoming consensus (raising $25M+ from a top-30 firm). These are not "bad decisions" but systematic blind spots that, when recognized in real-time, should trigger additional scrutiny before passing.

Pattern 1: "Category Is Sucky" / Static TAM Framing

MEDIUM PREDICTOR

What it looks like: Team sizes the TAM based on the company's current product and ICP, concludes the market is crowded or the wedge is too small. Fails to account for the company becoming the infrastructure layer that all competitors depend on.

Application to Rime: Voice AI is a hot but genuinely crowded space. Risk is that the framing becomes "just another TTS company competing with ElevenLabs" rather than "enterprise phonemic infrastructure layer that differentiates all voice systems." If sized as point solution, TAM looks $2-3B. If sized as infrastructure moat, TAM is $25B+.

55%

Pattern 3: Customer Signal Discounted by Market Thesis

MEDIUM PREDICTOR

Pattern 4: "Growth Is Too Recent" / Recency Discount

MEDIUM PREDICTOR

What it looks like: Company shows strong acceleration in last 3-6 months but team discounts it as "too early to tell." Prefers to wait for another quarter of data.

Why it predicts consensus: Waiting for more data at the Series A is the most expensive form of de-risking. The price will 2-3x by the time the next quarter confirms what the current quarter already shows.

Application to Rime: $1.5M in last 2 months of $3M total is exactly this pattern. 50% of all-time revenue concentrated in 60 days. This is the inflection point investors chase. "Let's wait and see next quarter" costs us the entry window.

Pattern strength for Rime:
65%

Pattern 5: Incumbent Response Over-Indexed

MEDIUM PREDICTOR

What it looks like: Team worries that OpenAI / Google / incumbent will build competitive functionality and eat the startup's lunch. Weighs theoretical incumbent response more heavily than observed reality.

Application to Rime: OpenAI and Google TTS exist but neither has built the phonemic accuracy layer for enterprise medical/brand terminology. Rime's moat is not against commoditized TTS but against competitors who need to handle regulated voice work. The competitive threat is real (Open AI could invest) but structural (phonemic pronunciation is R&D-intensive, not easy to copy).

Pattern strength for Rime:
50%

Pattern 7: Pricing Anchoring / "We Can Wait"

LOW PREDICTOR

What it looks like: Valuation at current round feels high relative to current metrics. Team assumes they can enter later at a better price.

Application to Rime: $25M round at likely $100-150M post for a company doing $3M ARR. At first glance, feels rich. But growth trajectory ($1.5M in 2 months) suggests this won't be the final price.
